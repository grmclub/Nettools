#most+common+copilot+prompts
The most common Microsoft Copilot prompts for 2026 focus on automating daily tasks, such as summarizing long email threads ("Summarize this long email..."), drafting meeting recaps, organizing files, and generating project status updates. Effective prompts include specific context, such as referencing files (e.g., /Q4-report.docx) and defining a clear tone

Top Common Copilot Prompts by Application

Email & Communication (Outlook/Teams):
	"Summarize this long email so I can reply quickly".
	"Draft a polite message to reschedule a meeting I forgot about".
	"Categorize my last 20 emails into: respond today, respond this week, FYI only, and delegate".
	"Draft a professional reply apologizing, proposing a next step, and offering a compensation option if appropriate".

Document Creation & Analysis (Word):

    "Summarize this document in bullet points".
    "Rewrite this paragraph in a more professional tone".
    "Draft a two-paragraph internal announcement about [policy/change] in a friendly, concise tone".
    "Create an executive summary of this report focusing on outcomes, risks, and recommended next steps".

Data & Analysis (Excel):

    "Create a chart that shows the relationship between marketing spend (column C) and lead generation (column E)".
    "Analyze January, February and March sales data and extract trends for the second quarter".
    "Turn this list of expenses into a basic budget".
	
Presentation Generation (PowerPoint):

    "Create a 10-slide outline for a presentation convincing skeptical managers to approve remote work policies".
    "Create a client presentation using our corporate template. Pull images from our SharePoint asset library".
    "Make slide 3 more visual. Add a timeline to slide 5".

General Productivity & Research:

    "Create a to-do list from this messy set of notes".
    "Explain what this error message means and how to fix it".
    "Help me set up a daily schedule based on these tasks".
    "Look at my emails and Teams messages from the past week about [project name]. Create a RAG status update".	
	
Key Tips for Better Prompts

    Be Specific: Instead of "write an email," use "Write a polite email to the team about the missed deadline".
    Provide Context: Mention the goal, audience, and source documents.
    Use Iteration: If the first result isn't perfect, tell Copilot to change the tone or add more details.

Key Elements of a High-Quality Copilot Prompt

    Goal: What do you want to achieve? (e.g., "Draft a proposal," "Summarize this document").
    Context: Why are you doing this? What is the background?.
    Source: What information should Copilot use? (e.g., Mention a specific file with / in Copilot chat).
    Persona: Tell Copilot how to act (e.g., "Act as a marketing manager").
    Format: How should the output look? (e.g., "Bullet points," "A table," "An email draft").
    Constraints: What should it avoid? (e.g., "Keep it under 5 sentences," "Exclude confidential data")

Examples of Effective Prompts

    Meeting Summary: Create a checklist of action items with owners and deadlines from a meeting transcript.
    Email Drafting: Generate a concise, professional reply to a specific email.
    Document Analysis: Analyze a document to identify missing information or unclear points.
    Image Generation: Use descriptive language to generate specific images for projects
