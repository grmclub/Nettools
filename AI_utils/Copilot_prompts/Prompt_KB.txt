1. Basic Exploration & Formatting
Get Copilot to aggregate and display raw data correctly over time. 

    "Visualize total [Revenue] by month using a line chart to highlight the overall trend."
    "Plot [Sales] over time. Add a rolling 7-day average trend line to smooth out daily fluctuations."
    "Can you resample this dataset and visualize [Profit] by quarter instead of by month using a bar chart?
	
2. Pattern & Seasonality Identification
Ask Copilot to highlight recurring trends or anomalies in your time series. 

    "Analyze my weekly sales figures for the past three years and look for recurring seasonal patterns or recurring spikes."
    "Generate a seasonal decomposition plot using Python code to isolate the trend, seasonality, and residual noise in my data."
    "Highlight and label any major outliers or anomalies on the monthly user-growth chart	

3. Forecasting & Predictive Visualization
Instruct Copilot to project future data and visualize the confidence intervals. 

    "Using the historical data, build an ARIMA predictive model for the next 12 months and plot the forecast with a 95% confidence interval."
    "What is the projected [Metric] for Q3 based on the last 2 years of data? Visualize it as a continuation of my current line chart.

4. Advanced Power BI / Python Copilot Prompts
Use these for deeper data science, faceting, and multi-variable comparison. 

    "Create a small multiples/facet grid to plot the daily [Temperature] over time, split by each [City]. Give each city's line a unique color."
    "Write a Python script to perform a stationarity test (ADF test) on my time series, print the results, and plot the rolling mean and standard deviation.

how to leverage Microsoft Copilot to transform raw data into compelling and actionable business narratives. The core message is that effective data storytelling requires moving beyond generic prompts toward a structured, strategic approach.
The Core Framework

To get high-quality outputs, the video suggests using a Context, Task, and Constraint framework (1:13):

    Context: Define your role and your audience.
    Task: Clearly state what the AI needs to achieve.
    Constraint: Set specific boundaries for format, length, or structure.

Top 10 Copilot Prompts for Data Storytelling

The video highlights 10 specific use cases to improve your workflow:

    1.Executive Summary Slide (1:34): Use a specific persona (e.g., Senior Business Analyst) to identify revenue drivers and outliers.
    2.Data Visualization (1:52): Move beyond simple charts by defining variables and highlighting ROI.
    3.Year-over-Year Performance (2:13): Analyze trends to understand why changes occurred.
    4.Team-Specific Insights (2:20): Tailor data for departments (like retention) to show business impact.
    5.Long-Term Trend Analysis (2:31): Identify seasonality and prepare for predictable dips (e.g., Q3 slowdowns).
    6.Actionable Recommendations (3:10): Force the AI to prioritize next steps rather than just describing data.
    7.Forecasting (3:19): Use historical patterns to predict future trends.
    8.KPI Clarity (3:29): Highlight the "Big Three" metrics using minimal text and bold visuals.
    9.Storytelling Structure (3:44): Use the Situation-Complication-Resolution (SCR) framework to guide the narrative.
    10.Closing with a Clear Ask (4:00): End presentations by summarizing risks and proposing a specific, confident action (like a budget increase).
